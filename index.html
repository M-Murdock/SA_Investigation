Key Information for You to Consider

Statement of Research: You are being asked to volunteer for a research study. It is up to you whether you choose to participate or not. There will be no penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled if you choose not to participate or discontinue participation.

Purpose: The purpose of this research is to evaluate how participants understand and interact and collaborate with an artificial intelligence (AI) system.

Duration: Your participation is expected to last approximately 30 minutes.

Procedures and Activities: You will be asked to provide input to a computer simulation using a keyboard. You will also be asked to provide overall feedback on the interaction via questionnaires.

Risks: The risks or discomforts associated with this research are minimal.

Benefits: There may be no personal benefit from your participation, but there may be potential benefits to improving human-robot collaborations.

What is this study about?

Researchers at Tufts University are conducting a study on how people understand and interact with AI in collaborative settings. You are being asked to participate because you have shown interest in the study. Your participation in the study is expected to last 30 minutes.

What will happen during this research?

If you agree to participate, you will do simple tasks — like moving a virtual spoon to an ingredient — in an online AI-driven cooking simulation using arrow keys on a keyboard. While you do the task, you may receive help from the online system. You will complete the task over 6 rounds; in each round the task and environment remain the same, but how the system attempts to help you will change. Following each round, you will answer a survey assessing your understanding of the agent's assistance and goals. You will also answer questions about your general background and your experience performing the tasks.

What will you do to protect my privacy?

We will take measures to protect your privacy and confidentiality. We will not collect your name or any other personally identifiable data. All data will be stored on a secure server with access limited only to the researchers of this study, except as required by individuals and organizations responsible for conducting or monitoring this research (including the Tufts SBER IRB). The results of the study may be published.

What is the compensation for the research?

You will receive compensation in the form of snacks or candy, provided after each session.

Is my participation voluntary, and can I withdraw?

Taking part in this research study is your decision. You do not have to take part, but if you do, you can stop at any time without penalty and without affecting your relationship with Tufts University, Dr. Elaine Short, or the research team. You have the right to choose not to participate in any study activity or to withdraw completely at any point. If you withdraw, data collected up to that point will be retained because it is non-identifiable and we will be unable to determine which data are yours.

Can I be removed from the research without my consent?

You may be removed from the study without your approval for reasons including intentionally antagonistic interactions meant to disrupt the study or unacceptable behaviors towards researchers.
